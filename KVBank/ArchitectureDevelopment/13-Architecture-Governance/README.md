# Architecture Development - Architecture Governance

- A system of controls over architecture
- A system to ensure compliance with architecture
- Effective management of those systems
- Accountability to the business for that
- Governance is layered within most organizations
    1. Corporate Governance (CEO, CIO, CFO)
    2. Technology Governace (CTO)
    3. Architecture Goverance

# Principles of Good Governance (specifically Enterprise wide Architecture Governance)

1. Discipline - all involved partites agree upon procedures and processes and respect the authoring of decision makers. Following the ordering of hierarch that this is the best system for the organization

2. Transparency - Create Architecture Principels that are clear and transparent instead of arbitaryness in decisions. There are no secret decisions

3. Independence - All processes, decisions avoid or minimize the conflicts, independent of goverance board is paraamount

4. Accountability - People who make decisions are authorized to do so and also authorized instead of publicly humiliating on the decisions. 

5. Responsbility - All people are involved are required to act responbily

6. Fairness - All decisions taken and all of the processes does not create unfair advantage to anyone (no business units partiality)


# Architecture Board

- Group of people that oversee the implemetation of the Architecture Strategy
- Representatives of all the key stakeholders in the architecture (change management, all decisions management, accountabiity, establhising certain targets like no of reuse/enforcement of architecture compliance, if implementation does not comply with the architecture, maturity level of architecture discipline)
- Meeting on regular basis
- Monitoring the architecture contract
- Review the policies and changes frequently and reflect back to business
- Cost savings key metrics that are tracking are actually met

# Architecture Contracts

- Joint agreement between development partners and sponsors on the delivarables, quality, and fitness-for-purpose of an architecture
